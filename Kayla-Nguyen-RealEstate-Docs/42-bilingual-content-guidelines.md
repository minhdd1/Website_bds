# Bilingual Content Guidelines

Project:
Kayla Nguyen Editorial Real Estate Website

---

## Core Principle

English content should NOT be a literal translation of Vietnamese.

The goal is:

* preserve meaning
* preserve emotion
* preserve brand personality

not word-for-word translation.

---

## Brand Voice

Vietnamese:

* nhẹ nhàng
* chân thành
* quan sát tinh tế
* giàu cảm xúc

English:

* calm
* thoughtful
* warm
* editorial
* human

---

## Example

Vietnamese:

"Một nơi khiến bạn muốn trở về."

Acceptable English:

"A place you'll love coming home to."

Not recommended:

"A place that makes you want to return."

---

## Brand Story

Use transcreation.

Editors may rewrite content to sound natural.

Maintaining emotional tone is more important than matching words.

---

## Living Journal

Journal articles should be written independently for each language when possible.

Avoid machine-translated articles.

---

## Area Guides

Vietnamese version:

Local lifestyle perspective.

English version:

Helpful context for expats and foreign residents.

---

## Property Listings

Property facts must be translated consistently.

Examples:

Phòng ngủ
→ Bedroom

Phòng tắm
→ Bathroom

Ban công
→ Balcony

Nội thất đầy đủ
→ Fully Furnished

Văn phòng hạng A
→ Grade A Office

---

## Kayla Notes

Kayla Notes should preserve personal voice.

Preferred:

Natural rewriting.

Avoid:

Literal translation.

---

## SEO Titles

Vietnamese:

Căn hộ 2PN Masteri Thảo Điền

English:

Masteri Thao Dien 2 Bedroom Apartment

---

## SEO Descriptions

Write separately for each language.

Do not auto-translate.

---

## Sanity Content Model

Localized fields:

title

excerpt

summary

content

seoTitle

seoDescription

Example:

{
"title": {
"vi": "Một buổi sáng ở Thảo Điền",
"en": "A Morning in Thao Dien"
}
}

---

## Translation Status

Each document should support:

draft
translated
reviewed

This helps content management.

---

## Editorial Rule

If a translation feels unnatural:

Rewrite.

Never sacrifice readability for literal accuracy.

The reader experience is more important than exact wording.

---

## Final Rule

The English website should feel like it was originally written in English.

The Vietnamese website should feel like it was originally written in Vietnamese.

Neither language should feel translated.
